    Universal property of the image

    We say i satisfies the universal property of the image of f if:

    $$ -‚àò(q,H) : hom_X(i, m) ‚Üí hom_X(f, m) $$

    For any $f : A ‚Üí X$ and $m : B ‚Ü™ X$, $hom_X(f, m)$ is a proposition.

    Image

    The image of a map $f : A ‚Üí X$ is a type which contains all $X$ which $f$ can map to, paired with the evidence that its preimage exists:

    $$ \text{im}(f) := Œ£_{(x:X)}||fib_f(x)|| $$
